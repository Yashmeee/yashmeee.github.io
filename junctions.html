Junctions to look out for

Cambridge is a city in the East of England, as seen on the map to the left.

  • The John Lewis Junction is dangerous if you are turning right, coming from the north, as there are often buses continuing straight on in the opposite direction. This is not obvious at first, so be careful not to turn into their path! Wait on your side of the road instead.
  • The Silver Street Junction is dangerous between 8.55am and 9.05am in particular, as a lot of students use this route to get to their lectures, which mostly start at 9am. Avoid this junction if possible and go for a different route, or be very careful when navigating around other cyclists and cars!
